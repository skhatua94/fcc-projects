Mother Teresa
Mother Teresa gives children her blessings at the Mother Teresa Church in Kolkata, India.

Religion

At age 18, Mother Teresa enrolled herself at the Institute of the Blessed Mary Virgin and became a nun. She took her very first religious vows in 1931. She taught at St. Mary's School after that. She created a religious community called Missionaries of Charity Sisters. She believed God asked her to do this. Their mission was to help those who were very sick and poor.

Charity

Mother Teresa created a school that became a home for those in very poor health. She also established the Missionaries of Charity. She also opened a home for orphans and homeless youths called Children's Home of the Immaculate Heart. Missionaries of Charity eventually created numerous hospices, orphanges, and leper houses all over India.

Awards

  • Nobel Peace Prize in 1979
  • John F. Kennedy International Award in 1971
  • Order of Merit in 1983
  • US Congressional Gold Medal in 1997
  • Pope XXII Peace Prize in 1971.
